a short poem

let's fall a bit further into the artist's sphere: a trove of lemon peel moons, rich oil paints, and tangled lines, i paint godly wounds spilling golden ichor, dip my palms into turpentine and let skin drink its thickness. in this world, pigment costs more than silver-- by weight, lightness is measured in brushstrokes.
